“Architecture is the process to express concrete elements composed of clear, transparent logic in the real world—nature, history, tradition and society—in an abstract spatial construction.”
--Tadao Ando

Project
韓松當代美術館（Museum San）
Architect
安藤忠雄（Tadao Ando）
Location
原州，南韓（Wonju, South Korea）
Year
2013

What distinguishes Tadao Ando’s Museum San from Richard Meier's Getty Center is how he diplomatically blended artifact and nature on this 275m-above-sea-level breathtaking site, where real-rock limestone vis-à-vis semi-rock concrete through a dubious yet bilingual approach. Whilst having us appreciate how he again made this hard-won cachet in architecture, Ando also trains our sight on the master artworks of Mark di Suvero, Alexander Liberman, Henry Moore, George Segal as well as James Turrell’s Sky Space, Horizon Room, Ganzfeld and Wedgework. This museum of paper, hosted via a Korean paper and chemical company called Hansol, reminds us of the power of writing, printing & making in the problematic era of social media


Ando, as ever, a lore de force!
